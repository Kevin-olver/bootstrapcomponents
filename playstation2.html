The PlayStation 2

Source: en.wikipedia.org https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_2

Sony Computer Entertainment is the company that created and markets the PlayStation 2.The first release dates were 4 March 2000 in Japan, 26 October 2000 in North America, 24 November 2000 in Europe, and 30 November 2000 in Australia. It is the PlayStation brand's second console release and the one that replaces the first PlayStation. It was in direct competition with Microsoft's Xbox and Nintendo's GameCube as a sixth-generation system. The PlayStation has sold more than 155 million units worldwide, making it the best-selling video game console of all time. Sony started creating the console in 1999 after the enormous success of its predecessor. The DualShock controller from the PlayStation 2's predecessor as well as its games were backwards compatible.The 128-bit "Emotion Engine," which Sony and Toshiba created specifically for the PlayStation 2, serves as the primary central processing unit (CPU) for the console. A 10-channel DMA unit, two Vector Processing Units (VPU), a memory controller, and an Image Processing Unit are among these components (IPU). The "Graphics Synthesiser" GPU is also specifically created for the console. It can render up to 75 million polygons per second at a fillrate of 2.4 gigapixels per second. With a clock speed of 150 MHz, the GPU also operates, and 4 MB of DRAM may transmit a display output of 1280 x 1024 pixels to PAL and NTSC televisions. A hard drive is necessary to play some games, most notably the well-known Final Fantasy XI, and can be inserted in the expansion bay on the back of the console. Additional DualShock or DualShock 2 controllers, a PlayStation 2 DVD remote control, a network adapter, an internal or external hard drive (HDD), horizontal and vertical stands, PlayStation or PlayStation 2 memory cards, the multitap for PlayStation or PlayStation 2, a USB motion camera (EyeToy), a USB keyboard and mouse, and a headset are all examples of optional hardware.
